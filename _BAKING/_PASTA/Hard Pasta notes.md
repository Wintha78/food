
### 1. Rich Egg Pasta Dough (4 Portions)

This dough is delicate and ideal for wide, silky noodles like **pappardelle**.

- **Flour:** 4 cups of Italian **"00" flour** (you can substitute all-purpose or bread flour if needed).
- **Eggs:** 4 large eggs (the rule of thumb is **one egg per person**).
- **Seasoning:** A fair amount of **salt**.

### 2. Hearty Semolina Pasta Dough (4 Portions)

This vegan-friendly, water-based dough is denser and perfect for capturing heavy sauces in shapes like **cavatelli**.

- **Flour:** 4 cups of **semolina flour**.
- **Water:** Approx. 1 1/3 cups of water.
- **Seasoning:** A hefty pinch of **salt**.
- _Note: If the dough feels too dry, add water in very small measures; if too wet, add a tiny bit more flour._

---

### Technique and Preparation

The "Nonna style" manual technique is recommended for both dough types:

1. **The Volcano:** Mound your flour on a clean surface and create a **large well** in the center—ensure it is big enough to hold your liquid without overflowing.
2. **Mixing:** Pour your eggs or water and salt into the well. Use a fork to whisk the liquid, then gradually incorporate flour from the inner walls of the "volcano" until the dough holds its own shape.
3. **Kneading:** Use your body weight to push the dough away from you, fold it in half, give it a quarter turn, and repeat. Continue until the dough is **elastic and smooth**.
4. **The Spring-Back Test:** Press your finger into the dough; if it **springs back**, the gluten has developed sufficiently. If the indentation stays down, keep kneading.
5. **The Rest:** Wrap the dough tightly in **plastic wrap** (or cover with a bowl or towel) and let it rest for **20 to 30 minutes** at room temperature. This allows the gluten to relax so the dough won't spring back when you try to roll it out.

---

### Shaping and Cooking

- **Cavatelli (Semolina):** Roll the dough into "snakes" and cut into small nuggets. Use your thumb, a butter knife, or an overturned fork to press and roll the dough, creating a cup shape with ridges to hold the sauce.
- **Pappardelle (Egg):** Roll the dough through a machine (from thickest setting 1 down to thin setting 7) or use a rolling pin. Flour the sheets well so they don't stick, roll them up, and cut into wide strips.
- **Cooking:** Always add pasta to **boiling, salted water**. Fresh pasta cooks very quickly—pappardelle may take only **2 to 3 minutes**.
- **The Secret Ingredient:** When moving pasta from the pot to the sauce, don't drain it completely. Use a little bit of the **starchy pasta water** to help the sauce stick to the noodles and give it a creamier consistency.
