To create pizza dough balls for freezing using **1000g of 00 flour and 70% hydration**, follow these steps:

### **1. Mixing and Developing Strength**

- **Initial Mix:** Combine the flour and 700g of water in a large bowl. If using fresh yeast, rub it into a portion of the flour until it crumbles before adding the water.
- **Adding Salt and Oil:** Once the dough starts to form a sticky mass, add approximately **25g of fine sea salt** and **30g of extra virgin olive oil**. Delaying the salt and oil allows the flour to hydrate more effectively.
- **Developing Gluten:** Turn the dough onto a work surface without extra flour. Use the **"stretch and arc" technique**: slide your fingers under the dough, scoop it up, swing it towards you, and slap it down, arcing the dough over itself to introduce air. Repeat until the dough becomes silky, bouncy, and no longer sticks to your hands.

### **2. Bulk Resting (Primary Fermentation)**

- **The First Rise:** Shape the dough into a smooth ball and place it in a clean, lightly oiled bowl.
- **Timing:** Cover the bowl with a damp cloth or a large plastic bag to prevent a skin from forming. Leave it in a "cosy" spot (22-24°C) for **3 to 6 hours**, or until it has increased by at least 50% or nearly doubled in volume.

### **3. Dividing and Balling**

- **Portioning:** Turn the rested dough onto a lightly oiled surface. Using a dough scraper and scales, divide the mass into individual pieces.
- **Quantity:** This recipe will yield approximately **6 to 7 pizza dough balls**, with each ball weighing roughly **200g to 250g**.
- **Shaping:** Take each piece and fold the edges into the center to create a "spine" of strength. Turn it over so the smooth side is up and roll it under your palm to create a tight, smooth ball.

### **4. Final Rest and Freezing**

- **Bench Rest:** Allow the shaped balls to rest on a tray for **30 to 60 minutes**. This allows the gluten to relax, which is essential for stretching the dough later without it springing back.
- **Freezing Process:** To freeze, lay the balls on a **flat tray** and place them in the freezer. Once they are frozen solid, transfer them into a **freezer bag** for long-term storage.
- **Usage:** When you are ready to make pizza, defrost the balls completely in the fridge or at room temperature before stretching them into bases. Do not shape them before freezing, as they become fragile and may break.
