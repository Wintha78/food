In sourdough baking, **autolyse** and **fermentation** are distinct but overlapping processes that prepare the dough for baking by developing its structure and flavor.

### Autolyse

**Autolyse** is the process of mixing only flour and water and letting the mixture rest (typically for 30 minutes to several hours) before adding the salt and sourdough starter.

- **Enzymatic Activity:** This rest period activates two key enzymes: **amylase**, which breaks starches into sugars, and **protease**, which breaks down complex proteins like gluten into simpler amino acids.
- **Benefits:** This technique gives the dough an "enzymatic head start," which improves the crumb's fluffiness, enhances crust browning, and makes the dough more extensible (stretchy).
- **Drawbacks:** The author of the sources notes that adding the starter and salt _after_ the flour is already hydrated can be cumbersome, often feeling like kneading from scratch. Because of this, he advocates for **fermentolysis**, which combines the benefits of autolyse and fermentation by mixing all ingredients at once but using a smaller amount of starter to slow down the process.

### Fermentation

**Fermentation** is the metabolic process where wild yeast and bacteria consume the sugars in the dough to produce carbon dioxide, alcohol, and organic acids.

- **The Microorganisms:**
    - **Wild Yeast:** Primarily responsible for the dough's "lift" or leavening. It produces carbon dioxide gas ($CO_2$), which gets trapped in the gluten matrix to create the "bubbles" (alveoli) in the bread.
    - **Bacteria (Lactic Acid Bacteria):** These outnumber yeast 100 to 1 and are responsible for the "sour" flavor of sourdough. They produce **lactic acid** (yogurty flavor) and **acetic acid** (vinegary flavor).
- **The Stages:**
    - **Bulk Fermentation:** The first rising period after all ingredients are mixed. This is the most critical stage for developing flavor and texture.
    - **Proofing:** The final rise that occurs after the dough has been shaped into a loaf. It inflates the dough one last time before it enters the oven.
- **Monitoring Progress:** Because fermentation speeds vary based on temperature and starter activity, the sources recommend monitoring the dough's progress using an **Aliquot jar** (a small sample to watch for size increase) or a **pH meter** (to measure acidity buildup) rather than relying on strict timings.

In summary, while autolyse focuses on **enzymatic preparation** of the flour, fermentation is the **biological life cycle** of the microorganisms that gives the bread its final structure, volume, and complex taste.
