Based on the sources, bread types can be categorized by their complexity, ranging from simple flatbreads that require no oven to freestanding sourdough loaves considered the "supreme discipline" of baking.

The following table lists the bread types found in the sources, categorized as **Easy** or **Hard** based on the required **Technique** and **Time**.

### Bread Types Difficulty Table

|Bread Type|Difficulty|Technique Measure|Time Measure|
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
|**Flatbreads** (Roti, Naan, Pea/Chickpea, Pancakes, Tortilla)|**Easy**|Very simple; involves mixing without the need for kneading, shaping, or an oven.|Extremely accessible; roughly 3 minutes of working time.|
|**Loaf Pan Bread** (Tin Loaf, White Sandwich, Pullman, Honey Whole Wheat)|**Easy**|Simple; the pan supports the dough as it rises, allowing the baker to skip complex shaping steps.|Efficient; roughly 5 minutes of working time.|
|**Basic French / White Bread**|**Easy**|Minimalist; requires only four basic ingredients and simple hand-mixing.|Standard prep; considered a "dead simple" introduction for new bakers.|
|**Cornbread**|**Easy**|Quick; ingredients are combined in a bowl and baked immediately in a skillet.|Very fast; no fermentation or rising time is required.|
|**Non-Wheat Sourdough** (Rye, Emmer, Einkorn)|**Easy to Medium**|Simpler; no gluten development (kneading) or complex shaping is needed.|Flexible; the dough is very forgiving of fermentation timing.|
|**Baguettes**|**Hard**|Challenging; requires mastering specific slashes with a lame and a multi-step shaping process.|Significant; bakers are advised to perfect simpler techniques before attempting these.|
|**Ciabatta & Fougasse**|**Hard**|Difficult; high hydration levels create a wet, "slack" dough that is very hard to handle.|Slow; requires building a ferment (biga) that rests for approximately 18 hours.|
|**Freestanding Wheat Sourdough**|**Hard**|Complex; involves precision in shaping (boule or batard), scoring for an "ear," and steam management.|High; requires around 60 minutes of active working time and 8–12 hours of balanced fermentation.|
|**Enriched Breads** (Challah, Babka, Brioche, Stollen)|**Hard**|Elaborate; involves intricate plaiting/folding and working with rich, very sticky dough.|High; involves multiple rises and careful temperature control to keep fats from seeping.|
|**High Hydration Pizza** (85%–100%)|**Hard**|Difficult; requires advanced "bassinage" techniques to incorporate water and machine-mixing to build strength.|Moderate to High; requires long bench rests to ensure the dough is elastic enough to shape.|

### Key Insights on Measures

- **Technique:** The presence of gluten is a major measure of difficulty. Doughs with high gluten (Wheat/Spelt) require kneading and shaping techniques to trap gases, whereas no-gluten flours (Corn, Chickpea) only need homogenization. Additionally, as hydration increases, the dough becomes stickier and more "complicated" to handle.
- **Time:** Slow fermentation is the "secret" to superior flavor and texture. While "Easy" breads can be made quickly, "Hard" breads often rely on long ferments (overnight or several days for sourdough) to break down complex carbohydrates and develop the gluten network.
